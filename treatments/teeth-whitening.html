What it is

Understanding teeth whitening.

Professional teeth whitening lightens the natural shade of your teeth using clinically approved whitening gels, supervised by a dental professional. At Vestadent this is typically provided as a custom take-home system and/or in-practice treatment, tailored to your teeth.

Because it's dentist-led, your gums and existing dental work are checked first, the strength is appropriate, and the process is monitored — which is safer and more predictable than over-the-counter or beauty-salon alternatives.

Who it's for

Is it right for you?

  • You'd like to lighten the natural colour of your teeth
  • Your teeth and gums are healthy (we'll check first)
  • You want a dentist-supervised, regulated treatment
  • You have realistic expectations of an even, natural brightness
  • You're 18 or over

The honest view

Benefits & what to consider.

Benefits

  • Dentist-supervised and regulated for safety
  • Custom-fitted trays for even results
  • Gums and existing dental work checked first
  • A brighter, more even natural shade
  • Guidance to maintain and top up the result

What to consider

  • In the UK, tooth whitening must legally be carried out under a dental professional
  • Whitening lightens natural teeth — it doesn't change crowns, veneers or fillings
  • Some temporary sensitivity is common and settles
  • Results vary between individuals and aren't permanent
  • It isn't suitable during pregnancy or under the age of 18
FAQ

Common questions.

Is teeth whitening safe?

When carried out or prescribed by a dental professional, whitening is a safe, regulated treatment. We check your teeth and gums first and supervise the process. By law in the UK, whitening must be done under a dentist.

Will it make my teeth sensitive?

Some people experience temporary sensitivity during whitening, which usually settles afterwards. We'll advise on how to minimise and manage it.

Will whitening work on crowns or veneers?

Whitening lightens natural teeth only — it won't change the colour of crowns, veneers or fillings. We'll factor this into your plan and discuss options.

How long do results last?

Results vary and aren't permanent; they can be maintained with occasional top-ups and good habits. Avoiding staining foods, drinks and smoking helps.

Can I use shop-bought whitening kits instead?

Over-the-counter and salon kits are not dentist-supervised and may be ineffective or unsafe. Dentist-led whitening checks your suitability and uses appropriately regulated products.
