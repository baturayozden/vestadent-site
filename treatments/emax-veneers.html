What it is

Understanding e.max veneers.

E.max veneers are thin shells of high-strength lithium-disilicate ceramic bonded to the front of your teeth. They're used to improve the colour, shape, size or alignment of the teeth that show when you smile.

Because the ceramic is translucent like natural enamel, well-made veneers blend in and catch the light naturally — while typically removing less tooth structure than a crown.

Who it's for

You may be suitable if

  • ✓You'd like to improve the colour, shape or spacing of your front teeth
  • ✓You have chips, worn edges or small gaps
  • ✓Your teeth are healthy but you're unhappy with their appearance
  • ✓You want a natural-looking, durable cosmetic improvement
  • ✓You maintain good oral hygiene

We'll also consider

  • !Some enamel may need to be prepared, which is irreversible
  • !Veneers can chip if misused (e.g. biting nails or hard objects)
  • !They don't change colour later, so the shade is chosen carefully upfront
  • !Not always the right option for heavy grinding — we'll advise
  • !Good hygiene and regular reviews protect your investment

Only an in-person assessment can confirm what's right for you — that's what your consultation is for.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do veneers ruin your teeth?

Veneers usually involve preparing a small amount of enamel, which is irreversible — so it's a considered, long-term choice. We only recommend them when appropriate and will discuss alternatives such as bonding where suitable.

Will they look natural?

Yes — E.max is translucent like natural enamel, and we design the shape and shade around your face and existing teeth. You'll preview the design before treatment.

How long do E.max veneers last?

With good care they can last many years. Longevity depends on your bite, habits and oral hygiene.

How many veneers will I need?

That depends on how many teeth show when you smile and your goals — anywhere from one to a full set. We'll recommend what looks balanced for your face.

How much do veneers cost?

Cost depends on the number of veneers and the work involved. We give you a clear written quote after your consultation.
