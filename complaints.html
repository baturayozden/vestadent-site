We aim to provide the highest standard of care to every patient. If something has not met your expectations, we want to know so we can put it right and improve. We treat all concerns seriously, in confidence, and without affecting your future care.

1. How to raise a concern

You can raise a concern in person, by telephone, by email or in writing. Please address complaints to our Complaints Manager:

2. How we will handle your complaint

  • We will acknowledge your complaint in writing within [TODO: e.g. 3] working days.
  • We will investigate the matter and aim to give you a full written response within [TODO: e.g. 10] working days. If we need more time — for example to speak with the clinician involved — we will let you know and keep you updated.
  • We will explain what we have found, what we will do, and offer to discuss the outcome with you.
  • We will keep a confidential record of your complaint and our response, separate from your clinical records.

3. If you are not satisfied

If you are not happy with our response, you can ask for the matter to be reviewed. You may also contact an independent body, depending on the type of treatment:

Private treatment

The Dental Complaints Service (DCS) provides a free, impartial service for resolving complaints about private dental care.

Dental Complaints Service
Telephone: 020 8253 0800
Website: dcs.gdc-uk.org

Professional conduct

The General Dental Council (GDC) regulates dental professionals in the UK. You can contact the GDC at gdc-uk.org.

Quality and safety of care

The Care Quality Commission (CQC) regulates dental practices in England. While the CQC does not resolve individual complaints, it welcomes information about people's experiences of care. You can contact the CQC at cqc.org.uk or on 03000 616161.

All complaints are handled confidentially and in line with our Privacy Policy. Making a complaint will never affect the quality of care you receive from us.
